Resources for ADHD

Living with ADHD can be challenging, but there are many organizations and resources available to help individuals and families affected by the condition. These resources provide support, education, and advocacy, and can make a significant difference in the lives of those affected by ADHD.

Organizations

  • CHADD (Children and Adults with Attention Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder): A national organization that provides education, advocacy, and support for individuals with ADHD and their families.
  • ADDitude Magazine: A leading publication that provides news, information, and resources for individuals with ADHD and their families.
  • ADHD Foundation: A UK-based organization that provides information, support, and advocacy for individuals with ADHD and their families.
  • Understood: A comprehensive online resource that provides information, support, and advocacy for individuals with ADHD and learning disabilities.
  • American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP): A professional organization that provides information and resources for parents and healthcare professionals on ADHD diagnosis, treatment, and management.

In addition to these organizations, there are many online communities and support groups available for individuals with ADHD and their families. These communities provide a safe and supportive space to connect with others who understand the challenges and triumphs of living with ADHD.

Online Communities and Support Groups

  • ADHD Support Group: A Facebook group with over 10,000 members that provides support, advice, and resources for individuals with ADHD and their families.
  • r/ADHD: A subreddit community with over 100,000 members that provides support, advice, and resources for individuals with ADHD.
  • ADD Forums: A online forum community that provides support, advice, and resources for individuals with ADHD and their families.

These organizations and online communities can provide a wealth of information, support, and resources for individuals with ADHD and their families. They can help individuals understand their diagnosis, develop strategies for managing symptoms, and connect with others who understand the challenges and triumphs of living with ADHD.

Hotlines and Helplines

  • CHADD Helpline: A toll-free helpline that provides information, support, and resources for individuals with ADHD and their families.
  • ADDitude Magazine Helpline: A toll-free helpline that provides information, support, and resources for individuals with ADHD and their families.

In addition to these resources, there are many other organizations, online communities, and support groups available to help individuals with ADHD and their families. By reaching out to these resources, individuals can find the support, information, and advocacy they need to thrive with ADHD.
